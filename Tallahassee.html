Tallahassee

Our state's bustling capital!

Quick Facts

  • Population: 201,731 (July 1, 2022)
  • Year of Incorporation: 1825
  • Region: Northern
  • Classification: Urban
  • Average Income: $52,899 (compared to state average, $67,917)

Tallahassee, as Florida's capital, has a significant role in governmental affairs. The Capitol Building serves as a the meeting place of the State, where history creating desicions are made. Simultaneously, the distinguished presence of Florida State University imparts an academic ambiance, contributing to the city's nuanced character defined by the merging of governance and collegiate activities.

Within the historical context, the Old Capitol Building stands as an archival testament, encapsulating the essence of bygone eras. Amidst the historical tapestry, the less-explored Maclay Gardens offers a haven of tranquility amidst lush greenery. While not overtly touristy, Tallahassee attracts those inclined towards the subtleties of governmental objectives or academic pursuits. The city's presence, marked by architectural legacies and beautiful natural retreats, resonates with a distinctive charm. It's also a great party town!
